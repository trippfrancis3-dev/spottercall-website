SPOTTERCALL

PRIVACY POLICY

Effective Date: April 1, 2026

SpotterCall (\"the App\") is a ballistic spotter calculator developed by Charles Tripp Francis. This Privacy Policy explains how we handle information when you use the App.

Information We Collect

SpotterCall is designed with your privacy in mind. The App collects minimal data to function:

  • Location Data: The App requests your approximate location (when you grant permission) solely to fetch local weather conditions from the Open-Meteo weather API. Your location is sent directly to Open-Meteo and is not stored by SpotterCall.
  • Microphone Access: The App uses your device microphone for voice-activated distance input via Apple's on-device Speech Recognition framework. All speech processing happens locally on your device. No audio recordings are stored or transmitted.
  • Ballistic Profiles: Any custom profiles you create are stored locally on your device only.

Information We Do Not Collect

SpotterCall does not collect, store, or transmit any of the following:

  • Personal identification information (name, email, phone number)
  • Device identifiers or advertising IDs
  • Usage analytics or tracking data
  • Audio recordings from the microphone
  • Precise GPS coordinates (only approximate location is used for weather)

Third-Party Services

The App uses the following third-party service:

  • Open-Meteo API (open-meteo.com): A free, open-source weather API used to fetch current atmospheric conditions based on your approximate location.

Data Storage

All app data is stored locally on your device. SpotterCall does not use any cloud storage, remote databases, or server-side accounts.

Data Sharing

SpotterCall does not share any data with third parties for advertising, analytics, or any other purpose.

Children's Privacy

SpotterCall does not knowingly collect information from children under 13.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Any changes will be reflected on this page with an updated effective date.
